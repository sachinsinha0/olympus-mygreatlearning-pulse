## The Opportunity

### Why This, Why Now

AI is moving faster than any curriculum can track. Every week, new tools, model capabilities, and workflows reshape how practitioners work — and how employers hire. Our learners know this. They are already consuming AI updates through YouTube, podcasts, and Twitter. But passive consumption does not build capability.

There is no structured product in the market that converts AI news into verified, applied learning — at speed, at scale, and with Great Learning's pedagogical rigour.

**Pulse fills that gap.** It is not a newsletter. It is not another YouTube channel. It is a biweekly learning experience — human-vetted, outcome-driven, and designed to keep every Great Learning AI program current.

### The gap we are closing

- AI developments are weekly. Curriculum updates are annual.
- YouTube is free but unstructured. Learning does not happen by watching.
- No competitor offers a human-vetted, outcome-mapped AI update product.
- Our own brand has lacked a standalone product worth subscribing to.

### Cadence

- **26 Releases / Year** — Biweekly cadence, always current
- **30–60 Min per Release** — Structured, outcome-driven learning

---

## Value Proposition

### What Pulse Is — and What Makes It Defensible

Pulse is differentiated on four dimensions that no YouTube channel, newsletter, or short-course platform can replicate together:

**01 — Structured Progression**
YouTube is a feed. Every Pulse release builds a coherent, dated record of what has mattered in AI — sequenced and scaffolded. After 12 months, a subscriber has a structured map of AI evolution that no video playlist can replicate.

**02 — The Human Vetting Gate**
No module ships unless a team member has personally used the tool or technique on a real, non-trivial problem. If we cannot demonstrate concrete value, we discard the topic. This editorial standard is a trust signal no algorithm-driven platform can offer.

**03 — Bloom's Arc in Every Release**
Each module moves the learner from awareness → understanding → application.

**04 — Compounding Archive Value**
Every release adds to a curated, searchable back-catalogue. A subscriber in month 13 inherits 26 structured modules covering the most significant AI developments of the year — a resource with no free equivalent.

### Why learners will keep subscribing

- They are building a personal, dated record of applied AI understanding — not just consuming content.
- The archive grows in value with every release — churn means losing access to a compounding asset.
- The product must simply continue to deliver one genuine "I learned something I actually used" moment per month.

## Success Metrics

### How We Define and Measure Success

Three primary KPIs govern Pulse.

| Metric | Year 1 Target | What It Signals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Completion Rate** | ≥ 60% | Learners who complete the hands-on demo within 7 days of release. Proxy for whether the content is compelling enough to finish — not just open. |
| **Watch Time** | ≥ 70% avg | Average % of video content watched per release. Drops below 50% on any single release trigger an immediate content review. |
| **Satisfaction Rating** | ≥ 4.2 / 5.0 | Post-module star rating. Below 3.8 on any release triggers a 48-hour team debrief. The target rises to 4.4 by release 10 as the format matures. |
